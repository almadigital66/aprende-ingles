Estilo Indirecto (Reported Speech)

Para contar lo que alguien más dijo, sin citarlo textualmente.

1. ¿Cómo funciona?

Cuando reportamos lo que alguien dijo, el tiempo verbal generalmente "retrocede" un paso hacia el pasado.

"I am happy" → She said she was happyPresente → Pasado simple
"I work here" → He said he worked therePresente → Pasado simple
"I will call" → She said she would callWill → Would

Ejemplos completos:

  • Direct: "I am tired." → Reported: She said she was tired.
  • Direct: "I like pizza." → Reported: He said he liked pizza.
  • Direct: "I will help you." → Reported: She said she would help me.
💡 Dato clave: también cambian los pronombres ("I" → "she/he") y las palabras de tiempo ("today" → "that day", "tomorrow" → "the next day").
